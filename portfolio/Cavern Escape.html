Cavern Escape

Cavern Escape is a small VR Game I developed alongside two artists over the course of a month. Our goal was to make a simple, one puzzle escape room with a creepy atmosphere.

Puzzle Design

To keep the scope of the game manageable, we limited ourselves to a single puzzle. I took the lead on designing the puzzle, and created a puzzle that required placing items in the correct order. Five stones with depictions of animals were placed in the level, but only three were required to complete the puzzle. To communicate which stones were to be placed in what order, clues were hidden in the level in the form of notes and cave paintings.

Flashlight

Because Cavern Escape takes place in a dark cave, we included a flashlight so the player can find their way in the dark. Rather than have the flashlight permanently attached the player's hand, we allowed them to pick up and drop the flashlight as they wished. To ensure the player was only able to turn the flashlight on or off while holding it, Unreal's enhanced input system was used, to load and unload the flashlight specific controls from their player controller. Additionally, to make sure the player didn't lose the flashlight, an in game UI widget was attached to it that would appear only when the player let go of the flashlight.
